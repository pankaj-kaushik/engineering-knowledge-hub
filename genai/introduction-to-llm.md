## 2. What is a Large Language Model (LLM)?

**Definition (Simple):**\
A Large Language Model is a computer system trained on massive amounts
of text so it can **understand and generate human-like language**.

**Real-World Analogy:**\
Think of an LLM as a **super-smart autocomplete brain** that has read
millions of books.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. How LLMs Work (High Level --- Non-Math)

LLMs are trained on huge collections of text and learn **language
patterns**.

**Tokens:** Small pieces of text processed by the model.

## 5. Key Concepts Every Beginner Must Know

-   Tokens --- pieces of text processed by model
-   Prompt --- instruction given to model
-   Embeddings --- numeric representation of meaning
-   Fine-tuning --- training model on domain data
-   Context Window --- how much text model can remember
-   Hallucination --- incorrect generated information
-   Temperature --- controls randomness
